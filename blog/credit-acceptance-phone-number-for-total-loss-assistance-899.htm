Understanding Total Loss and Its Impact

What Is a Total Loss?

A total loss occurs when the cost of repairing a vehicle exceeds its actual cash value (ACV). Insurance companies typically declare a vehicle a total loss after an accident, theft, or natural disaster. For many, this is a devastating financial blow, especially if the car is essential for work, family, or daily life.

The Emotional and Financial Toll

Losing a vehicle isn’t just an inconvenience—it can disrupt livelihoods. In 2023 alone, extreme weather events like hurricanes and wildfires led to a surge in total loss claims. For those already struggling with inflation or job instability, a total loss can push them into deeper financial stress.
